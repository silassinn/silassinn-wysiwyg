What to Expect

Everything you need to know before, during, and after your session — so you can arrive confident and get the most out of your appointment.

First Visit

Your First Session with Ollie

New to therapeutic massage, or just new to working with Ollie? Here's exactly what to expect from your first appointment.

1

Arrival & Welcome

Arrive 10–15 minutes early for your first visit. You'll complete a brief health intake form covering your medical history, current complaints, and treatment goals.

2

Intake Consultation

Ollie will review your intake form with you, ask follow-up questions about your pain patterns, movement history, and any specific areas of concern. This usually takes 10–15 minutes.

3

Postural Assessment

For most clients, Ollie will perform a brief standing postural assessment and/or range-of-motion screening to identify structural patterns before treatment begins.

4

Treatment Session

You'll undress to your comfort level, be professionally draped throughout, and the hands-on portion of your session begins. Ollie explains techniques as he works and checks in regularly on pressure.

5

Post-Session Review

After the session, Ollie will share his findings, recommendations for frequency, and any self-care stretches or movement tips to reinforce the work between visits.

6

Scheduling Follow-Up

If ongoing treatment is recommended, Ollie will suggest a schedule. No pressure — recommendations are based on what he genuinely believes will produce the best outcome for you.

Time note: The intake and assessment add approximately 15–20 minutes to your first visit. For a 60-minute session, plan for about 80 minutes total for the first appointment.
Health Intake

The Intake Form

The health intake form is a critical part of effective therapeutic massage. It helps Ollie understand your full health picture — not just where it hurts — so he can work safely and effectively.

You'll be asked about:

  • Current medications
  • Medical conditions and surgical history
  • Areas of pain, tightness, or restriction
  • Recent injuries or illnesses
  • Activity level and sport(s)
  • Previous massage experience
  • Treatment goals for this session

All information is kept strictly confidential per HIPAA guidelines and used exclusively to guide your treatment.

Contraindications

When NOT to Book

Massage is contraindicated in certain situations for your safety. Please reschedule if you have:

  • Active fever or contagious illness
  • Open wounds or skin infections in treatment areas
  • Recent surgery (within 6 weeks) without physician clearance
  • Deep vein thrombosis (blood clots) — unmanaged
  • Active cancer without physician clearance
  • Severe osteoporosis

If you're unsure whether your condition is a contraindication, call or email first — Ollie is happy to advise without any obligation to book.

Preparation

How to Prepare for Your Session

Hydrate

Drink plenty of water in the hours before your session. Well-hydrated tissue responds better to massage and recovers faster afterward.

Eat Light

Avoid heavy meals 1–2 hours before your appointment. A light snack is fine — lying face-down on a full stomach is uncomfortable.

Dress Comfortably

You'll undress to your level of comfort. Ollie uses professional draping at all times — only the area being worked is exposed. Wear something easy to remove and put back on.

After Your Session

Post-Session Self-Care

The 24–48 hours after a therapeutic massage session are critical for getting maximum benefit from the work.

Do This After Your Session

  • ✓
    Drink 2–3 extra glasses of water — therapeutic massage stimulates lymphatic flow and releases metabolic waste from the tissue. Water helps flush this out.
  • ✓
    Rest if possible — especially after deep tissue or neuromuscular work. Your nervous system and tissue benefit from low-demand activity for the next few hours.
  • ✓
    Apply heat to sore areas — if you experience soreness, a heating pad or warm bath/shower helps relax the tissue and increase comfort.
  • ✓
    Do your assigned stretches — Ollie often provides targeted stretches at the end of sessions. Doing them within 24 hours while the tissue is primed produces better results.

What to Avoid

  • ✗
    Intense exercise — avoid hard training for 12–24 hours after deep tissue or neuromuscular work. Light walking is fine.
  • ✗
    Alcohol — alcohol and massage both dehydrate you. Combining them increases dehydration and can worsen soreness.
  • ✗
    Ice on worked areas — ice constricts the blood flow that's been stimulated by the massage. Use heat, not ice, for soreness after a session.
  • ✗
    Ignoring unusual pain — mild soreness (like post-workout soreness) is normal for 24–48 hours. Sharp, shooting, or worsening pain is not — contact Ollie immediately.

Neuromuscular Therapy

A clinical, evidence-based approach to identifying and eliminating the root causes of chronic pain — not just managing symptoms.

What It Is

Finding the Source, Not Just the Symptom

Neuromuscular therapy (NMT) is a specialized form of soft tissue manipulation that addresses the neurological component of musculoskeletal pain. The premise is simple but powerful: muscle pain often isn't caused by what hurts. A trigger point — a hyperirritable spot in a muscle — can create referred pain patterns that mimic other conditions, send pain to locations far from the trigger itself, and persist despite conventional treatment.

Ollie's NMT approach begins with a detailed intake and postural assessment. He maps your pain patterns, identifies postural distortions and muscular imbalances, and pinpoints the trigger points responsible for your symptoms. Then, using precise sustained pressure, cross-fiber friction, and targeted stretching, he deactivates those trigger points and restores normal neuromuscular function.

This is clinical work — methodical, anatomy-driven, and results-oriented. Clients who've tried everything often find that NMT addresses the underlying pattern that other interventions missed.

Core Techniques

What Happens in a Neuromuscular Session

1

Intake & Assessment

Comprehensive pain history, postural analysis, and range-of-motion testing to map the dysfunction before treatment begins.

2

Trigger Point Mapping

Systematic palpation to locate active and latent trigger points in the muscles contributing to your pain pattern.

3

Trigger Point Release

Sustained ischemic compression and positional release techniques deactivate trigger points and restore normal muscle tone.

4

Myofascial Release

Fascial restrictions surrounding the muscles are addressed to restore mobility and prevent recurrence.

5

Corrective Stretching

Targeted stretches for shortened muscles and strengthening recommendations for their lengthened antagonists.

6

Home Care Plan

Self-care stretches, postural awareness cues, and activity modifications to reinforce session outcomes between visits.

Clinical note: Neuromuscular therapy is most effective as a series of sessions. For chronic conditions, Ollie typically recommends 3–6 sessions over 4–6 weeks for a thorough reassessment. Many clients notice meaningful improvement after the first 2–3 sessions.

Sports Recovery Massage

Performance-focused massage for Bellingham's athletes, trail runners, cyclists, hikers, and outdoor adventurers.

Designed for Athletes

Train Harder. Recover Faster. Perform Better.

Sports recovery massage is not just massage with a different name — it's a purposeful integration of techniques selected specifically for athletes and active adults who put consistent, high demands on their bodies. Whether you're a competitive runner logging 60+ miles per week, a cyclist grinding through mountain passes, a hiker tackling the North Cascades, or a weekend warrior who refuses to slow down, your body has specific recovery needs that a general relaxation massage simply doesn't address.

Ollie built his sports massage expertise working with elite and recreational athletes in Jackson Hole, Wyoming — one of the most active outdoor communities in North America. He understands the physiological demands of endurance sports, the muscle groups most vulnerable to overuse injury, and how to structure a session around your current training phase for maximum benefit.

Sessions typically combine effleurage and petrissage to stimulate circulation, compression to flush metabolic waste from fatigued muscles, friction techniques to break up adhesions in commonly stressed tissues, and active-assisted stretching to restore flexibility and joint range of motion. The blend depends entirely on where you are in your training cycle and what your body needs right now.

Session Types

Pre-Event vs. Post-Event vs. Maintenance

Pre-Event (24–72 hrs before)

Light, stimulating strokes to increase circulation and neuromuscular activation without fatiguing the muscles. Ideal before races or major events. Does NOT include deep tissue work — that's saved for recovery.

Post-Event (24–72 hrs after)

Flush lactic acid and metabolic waste, reduce inflammation, and begin tissue repair. Gentle to moderate pressure timed to minimize delayed-onset muscle soreness (DOMS).

Maintenance (between events)

The most comprehensive session — combines deep tissue work, trigger point release, and assisted stretching to address overuse patterns and prevent injury during training blocks.

The Process

How a Maintenance Sports Session Works

1

Sport & Training Review

Ollie asks about your sport, current training load, upcoming events, and any recurring issues or tight areas.

2

Range of Motion Check

Quick functional movement screen to identify restrictions and prioritize the session's focus areas.

3

Targeted Recovery Work

Sport-specific focus: runners get hamstrings, calves, and IT band; cyclists get hip flexors and low back; climbers get forearms and thoracic spine.

4

Active-Assisted Stretching

Proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation (PNF) stretching to achieve flexibility gains beyond passive stretching alone.

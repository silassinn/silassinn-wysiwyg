Deep Tissue Massage

Targeted, clinical deep tissue work for lasting relief from chronic pain, tension, and restricted movement — Bellingham, WA.

What It Is

More Than Just Firm Pressure

Deep tissue massage is often misunderstood as simply "hard massage." In reality, it's a precise therapeutic technique that requires detailed anatomical knowledge, the ability to palpate individual muscle layers, and the skill to apply targeted pressure at exactly the right angle and depth to create meaningful change in soft tissue.

Ollie uses slow, deliberate strokes with reinforced thumbs, knuckles, and forearms to work through the superficial layers and access the deeper muscles — the ones that conventional massage never reaches. This is where chronic tension, scar tissue, and the root causes of persistent pain hide.

Unlike a spa deep tissue session that simply applies more pressure, Ollie's approach is outcome-focused: he assesses your posture, range of motion, and pain patterns before the session begins, then adapts his technique in real time based on how your tissue responds. The result is lasting structural change rather than temporary relief.

Who Benefits Most

Deep tissue massage is particularly effective for clients experiencing:

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Stiff neck & shoulders
  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Repetitive strain injuries
  • Scar tissue from past injuries
  • IT band syndrome
  • Postural tension from desk work
  • Recovery from minor muscle tears
What to Expect

Your Deep Tissue Session

1

Assessment

Ollie conducts a brief postural and movement assessment to identify the key areas driving your pain or restriction.

2

Warm-Up Work

The session begins with lighter effleurage to warm the tissue and increase circulation before deeper work begins.

3

Deep Tissue Techniques

Targeted stripping, cross-fiber friction, and compression work through the layers of muscle tissue and fascia.

4

Integration & Stretching

The session closes with integration strokes and passive stretching to reinforce the work and improve range of motion.

Intensity note: Deep tissue work can feel intense in areas of chronic tension. Ollie communicates throughout and adjusts pressure to stay within your therapeutic range. Some clients experience 24–48 hours of mild soreness afterward — this is normal and indicates the body is responding and remodeling.

About Ollie Tripp

Licensed Massage Therapist | 13+ Years Experience | Bellingham, WA

Background & Experience

Expert Hands Built Over 13 Years

Ollie Tripp discovered the power of therapeutic massage as a competitive athlete who spent years training in the mountains of the American West. After experiencing firsthand how the right bodywork could mean the difference between a sidelined season and a personal best, he committed himself to learning the craft at the highest level.

He completed his initial massage therapy training in [School Name], graduating with honors, and immediately pursued advanced certification in neuromuscular therapy and sports massage — the two modalities he found most impactful for athletic and active populations.

For the better part of a decade, Ollie built his practice in Jackson Hole, Wyoming — a community of elite skiers, mountain bikers, ultramarathoners, and outdoor athletes who demand exceptional performance from their bodies year-round. Working in that environment sharpened his clinical instincts and expanded his toolkit far beyond what most massage therapists encounter in a conventional practice.

Now based in Bellingham, WA, Ollie brings that same level of expertise and passion to one of the Pacific Northwest's most active communities. Whether you're preparing for a Cascade trail race, managing the physical demands of a desk career, or working through a nagging injury that's kept you off the trails — Ollie's approach is always the same: honest assessment, skilled hands, and a genuine investment in your outcome.

Treatment Philosophy

How Ollie Approaches Every Session

"I'm not interested in making you feel good for an hour. I'm interested in making you move and feel better for weeks."

Ollie's philosophy begins with listening — not just to what a client says, but to what their tissue is telling him through palpation. Pain is a signal, not a destination, and his job is to trace that signal back to its source.

He believes massage therapy is most effective when it's treated as a collaborative process. Clients who understand what's being done, why it's being done, and how to reinforce the work at home get dramatically better results than those who show up, check out for an hour, and hope for the best.

He also believes in honesty. If a condition requires more than massage — physical therapy, chiropractic care, medical attention — Ollie will tell you. His goal is your outcome, not your ongoing dependency on his schedule.

The Jackson Hole years shaped this approach. Working with athletes who couldn't afford to miss events, who needed maximum results from each session, and who asked excellent questions pushed Ollie to develop a level of clinical precision that most massage practices don't require. That standard came with him to Bellingham, and it informs every session he does.

A Differentiator Worth Noting

The Jackson Hole Years

Jackson Hole, Wyoming is not a typical massage market. It's home to some of the country's most elite and most demanding athletes — professional skiers, ultramarathon champions, professional climbers, and serious competitive cyclists who train year-round in a high-altitude, high-consequence environment.

Serving that community for years taught Ollie things about sports physiology, injury patterns, and recovery optimization that simply aren't available in a textbook or a classroom. It also taught him how to be direct, efficient, and results-focused — because athletes don't have time for anything less.

That background is what makes his Bellingham practice different. Bellingham's outdoor community is among the most active in the Pacific Northwest — trail runners, mountaineers, cyclists, kayakers, and climbers who deserve a massage therapist who truly understands what their bodies go through.

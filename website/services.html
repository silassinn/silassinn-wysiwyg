Therapeutic Massage Services

Specialized modalities for athletic performance, pain relief, and whole-body wellness — with transparent pricing.

Service 01

Deep Tissue Massage

Deep tissue massage targets the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue using slow, deliberate strokes and firm pressure. It's one of the most effective tools for breaking up scar tissue, releasing chronic muscle tension, and addressing musculoskeletal pain that hasn't responded to lighter modalities.

Ollie's deep tissue work is precise rather than just heavy-handed — he identifies the specific layers and structures creating your problem before applying targeted pressure that creates lasting change rather than temporary relief.

  • Chronic back & neck pain
  • Post-workout soreness
  • Scar tissue breakdown
  • Postural correction
  • Repetitive strain injuries

Service 02

Neuromuscular Therapy

Neuromuscular therapy (NMT) is a clinical, evidence-based approach to soft tissue pain that goes beyond symptom relief. By analyzing posture, movement patterns, and muscular imbalances, Ollie identifies the underlying causes of pain rather than just treating where it hurts.

Core techniques include trigger point release (deactivating hyperirritable muscle knots), myofascial release, and targeted stretching protocols. Clients with chronic headaches, sciatic pain, and postural dysfunction often see dramatic improvements where other approaches have failed.

  • Trigger point release
  • Sciatic pain relief
  • Chronic headaches
  • TMJ dysfunction
  • Postural imbalances

Service 03

Sports Recovery Massage

Athletes and active adults who consistently push their limits need a recovery strategy that matches their ambition. Sports recovery massage combines multiple techniques — effleurage, petrissage, compression, and assisted stretching — to flush metabolic waste, reduce inflammation, and restore muscle elasticity between training sessions.

Whether you're prepping for race day or recovering from a hard training block, Ollie structures sessions around your specific sport and current training phase for maximum benefit.

  • Pre-event preparation
  • Post-event recovery
  • Injury prevention
  • Flexibility enhancement
  • Performance maintenance

Frequently Asked Questions

Ollie does not currently bill insurance directly. However, he can provide detailed receipts that may qualify for FSA/HSA reimbursement. Some clients also receive partial reimbursement through supplemental health plans — check with your provider.

Deep tissue massage focuses on chronic tension and structural change within specific muscle groups. Sports massage is more comprehensive — it incorporates multiple techniques timed around your training schedule to optimize performance and accelerate recovery. In practice, Ollie blends both based on your needs.

Undress to your comfort level — you'll be professionally draped throughout the session. Avoid heavy meals 1–2 hours before. Arrive hydrated. For sports recovery clients, avoid hard training the same day as your session when possible.

Therapeutic massage can feel intense — especially deep tissue and trigger point work — but should never exceed a "good hurt" (discomfort that feels productive, not sharp or unbearable). Ollie continuously checks in on pressure and adjusts to stay within your therapeutic range. Always communicate if something feels wrong.

It depends on your goals. For acute pain or injury recovery, 1–2 sessions per week initially is common. For maintenance and performance, most athletes benefit from bi-weekly sessions. For general wellness, monthly sessions are a solid baseline. Ollie will give you an honest recommendation after your first session.
